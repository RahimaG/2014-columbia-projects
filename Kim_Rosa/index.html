McDonald's Incident in Flushing Highlights Need for Social Spaces for Seniors

From left, Young Jin Kim, Christine Colligan, Stephen Kim and Mi Shim outside a McDonald's in Queens that they say has disrespected Koreans.

Michael Appleton for The New York Times

Location

144-01 Northern Blvd. Flushing, Queens

Timeline

  1. Jan. 3: Local police are called to ask seniors at a Flushing McDonald's on the intersection of Parsons and Northern Boulevards to leave after the management said they stayed beyond the 20 minutes allowed.
  2. Jan. 16: Community leaders urge Flushing residents to boycott the restaurant.
  3. Jan. 20: Assemblyman Ron Kim and U.S. Rep. Grace Meng reach a deal with the restaurant to ease the 20 min. sitting rule during off-peak hours.

Voices

I will just listen to them, but I will come back inside after they leave. - Sang Yong Park, 76, McDonald's patron

It's a McDonald's, not a senior center. - Martha Anderson, general manager

We had to dig deeper to comprehend the pride of a small business owner as well as the pride of our seniors to seek a place to socialize. - Assemblyman Ron Kim
